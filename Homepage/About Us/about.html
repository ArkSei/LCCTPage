INNOVATIONS & REMARKABLE ACHIEVEMENTS (SY 2012-2013)

K-12 IMPLEMENTATION

Ms. Remedios Molinyawe, along with other academic administrators, began the implementation of the K-12 program, adding foreign languages such as Spanish and Nihongo.

PAASCU LEVEL II STATUS (2012-2013)

PAASCU Level II Accreditation of LCCT

The Grade School and High School Departments were granted Level II re-accreditation, ensuring another 5 years of Level II status.

UbD Implementation (SY 2010-2011)

Understanding by Design

Ms. Remedios Molinyawe and Mrs. Erlinda Padilla began implementing the UbD (Understanding by Design) framework.

Emerald Jubilee (2008)

Sapphire Year of LCCT

Sr. Emilia Lacuarta, OSA, focused on achieving innovative education and professional growth for the entire personnel.

College Department - First Graduation Rites (March 31, 2007)

LCCT Higher Education First Commencement Exercises

The College Department celebrated its first graduation with 13 graduating students from various courses.

PAASCU VISIT (2003)

PAASCU Accreditation

LCCT passed the PAASCU accreditation for both the High School and Grade School Departments.

BUILDING CONSTRUCTION (2002-2003)

Ground-breaking and Construction of New Building

A four-storey building was constructed to house the High School and College Departments, along with the approval of several tertiary courses by CHED.

Golden Jubilee (1998-1999)

OLFA Golden Jubilee Celebration

The school celebrated its Golden Jubilee with a week-long celebration.

Continual Improvement for Stability and Growth (1988-1994)

New Principal and New Buildings

Sister Concepcion Leopoldo, OSA, oversaw the construction of new buildings, including a multi-purpose hall and gymnasium with support from the high school graduates.

Revolutionizing the Catholic-Augustinian Education (1978-1985)

Adoption of Catholic School System Development Program

Sister Ma. Fidelis encouraged the adoption of the CS-SD program to promote staff development and improve educational standards.

Revitalizing Students' Potential (1970-1978)

Focus on Holistic Student Development

Sisters Susana Agudelo, OSA, and Adelina Segismundo, OSA, re-engineered the school activities to emphasize holistic student development. The OLFA Alumni Association was also launched in 1973.

Transforming the Barung-Barung School into OLFA (1948-1970)

Inauguration of OLFA School

On October 24, 1948, the school building housing the pioneer students of OLFA was inaugurated, starting with three classrooms and a small library.

The Church and Augustinian Education (1948)

Establishment of Catholic Schools in the Philippines

The Augustinian Friars began establishing Catholic schools, including OLFA, to provide educational services to local communities.

Our Lady of Fatima (OLFA) was Established (June 20, 1948)

Founding of OLFA

Sor Encarnacion de los Remedios, OSA, and three other nuns founded OLFA with the aim of providing quality education to the local community.
